    FeelLove Coffee brings quality, ‘eclectic’ coffee shop to downtown Loveland

    New store takes the spot of former Coffee Tree

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
    By Austin Fleskes | afleskes@prairiemountainmedia.com
    PUBLISHED: September 29, 2021 at 5:07 PM MST

    Loveland’s newest coffee stop brings quality ingredients with a unique style together to offer the Sweetheart City’s residents a new place to get a cup of joe.

    FeelLove Coffee celebrated its grand opening Saturday (Sept. 25) after several months of renovations and changes inside the building that formerly housed the Coffee Tree.

    Owners Tanner and Snowee Wood brought the family business to Colorado from Utah, where Tanner said his mom and stepdad own five FeelLove Coffee locations, including one at the entrance to Zion National Park.

    Tanner Wood said that when the pair would visit his parents in Utah, they would always jump at the chance to go and get some coffee.

    “We always thought ‘we can’t wait to go to FeelLove,'” Tanner Wood said.

    The pair said that for years the plan was to bring a FeelLove location to Colorado, but they wanted to find the right spot for it. While they looked in Estes Park, Fort Collins and even other areas of downtown Loveland, they found the former Coffee Tree to be exactly what they were looking for.

    They offered to buy the location from the Coffee Tree owners last summer, but the offer was declined and the search for the right FeelLove location continued. However, in January of this year the pair returned to the Coffee Tree owners to see if they had changed their minds; they had, and the Woods bought the location.

    Tanner Wood said after the purchase they continued to run the store as the Coffee Tree for several months so they could get to know the community and what they liked.

    “We wanted people to continue their day as normal,” Tanner Wood said.

    In June, the store closed to begin renovations to bring the FeelLove feeling to its first Colorado location.

    Portions of the floor were replaced with hexagonal tiles, a new bar was put in, the ceilings were redone with gold, square tiles and new furniture was brought in to bring forth the “eclectic, venetian” style that Snowee Wood now describes. A tile mosaic of a tree was placed right in the center of the front counter so the legacy of the Coffee Tree could continue.

    The pair said construction and certifications were done right up until the day before their grand opening. But come last Saturday, the new coffee stop was able to welcome an eager, caffeine-hungry community back in.

    “We felt so much love from the community,” Snowee Wood said. “I felt honored.”

    “To be able to see the amount of people who came out to support us (was great),” Tanner Wood added.

    Now open, the Wood family is looking to bring unique, quality coffee and food to the people of Loveland.

    Tanner Wood said that one of the things that set them apart is who they are compared to corporate competition.

    “We are not a Starbucks,” Tanner Wood said. “People are wanting to support local.”

    Snowee Wood said the store specializes in quality prepared coffee, brewing their array of drinks with Oatly oat milk and using extracts to sweeten. Tanner Wood added the way they make their drinks sets them apart, focusing on quality ingredients, from the coffee itself to the milk used to make it even smoother, to make a delicious cup of coffee.

    “We really focus in on the health aspect of our coffee,” Snowee Wood said.

    Tanner Wood added they also offer top-tier food from an in-house pastry chef and five-star chef who prepares a selection of hot dishes. He said it is difficult to put into words how great things taste until someone tries it for themselves; drinks like the Buddy Holly, a house crafted cold brew that is lightly sweetened with white chocolate and organic agave, a touch of hazelnut and a splash of Oatly or half and half, or the Honey Boo made with 100% Kona Hawaiian espresso, organic honey and cinnamon.

    While only open for a few days, the shop was bustling with customers, many of whom tried FeelLove for the first time Wednesday. Loveland residents Damien Mallette and Sarah Kuykendall were two such customers. Mallette described his drink and the location as mind blowing; he added it was so nice inside he jokingly thought “we probably shouldn’t be here.”

    “They clearly put so much money into it and…it was so good,” Mallette added.

    With just under a week under their belt, the Woods are ready to serve the Loveland community.

    “There’s something special when you walk in,” Tanner Wood said. “And that is what we wanted to create.”

    FeelLove Coffee is open from 6 a.m. to 6 p.m. every day in downtown Loveland, 210 E. Fourth St.
